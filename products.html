Shellfish

What are shellfish?

Shellfish is a colloquial term for just about any hard shelled (exoskeleton) bearing invertebrate, that lives in the water. Some examples include, clams, mussels, crabs and lobsters.

Groundfish

What are groundfish?

Groundfish, also known as demeral fish, live and feed on or near the bottom of seas or lakes. They occupy the sea floors and lake beds, which usually consist of mud, sand, gravel or rocks.

Pelagic

What are pelagic fish?

Pelagic fish are fish inhabit the open and free waters of the ocean (known as the pelagic zone). Neither close to the surface or the bottom. Marine life here can swim freely without any boundary constraints.
